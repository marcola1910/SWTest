A fire suddenly broke out on an uninhabited island. 


This uninhabited island has the shape of a two-dimensional grid of N rows and M columns, and some grids have forests. The fire spreads up, down, left and right, and burns for as long as the density of the forest. 0 means sand and does not catch fire.


The time the fire spreads takes one unit time. Let’s find out the largest possible area the fire will occupy. 


[Figure]


[Figure] is an example of N=3 and M=3.


If the fire started at (2,1), the initial fire area is 1.


If the fire spreads over time, the fire area becomes 3. The fire spreads thereafter, however, since the places where the fire had occurred are burnt out and the fire is extinguished, 3 therefore is the largest fire area when time is 2. 


Given the information of the uninhabited island and the location of the fire, calculate the area size and the time of the largest fire. If there are multiple fires of the same size, choose the one that reaches maximum size the fastest. 


[Constraints]

1.N, the row and M, the column are integers from 1 and 200, including 1 and 200.

2. K, the number of areas where fires break out first is an integer from 1 to 50, including 1 and 50. 

3. The density of the forest is an integer from 1 to 100, including 1 and 100. 

4. Ash after forest burnt does not catch a fire.

5. The first fire broke out in other forests simultaneously. 


[Input]

The number of test cases (T) is given, followed by T test cases. N, the row, M, the column and K, the number of areas where fires break out are given as spaces. Starting from the line 2 to N+1, M number of information of uninhabited island are given. From -N+2 to line K, the row and the column of areas where fire broke out are given as spaces. 


[Output]

Print each test case to a separate line. Print #x (x=Test case number, beginning from 1), followed by a space. Print the time and the size of the largest fire area. If there are multiple fires of the same size, choose the one that reaches maximum size the fastest.
